{% else if page == "faq" %}

What is a haplogroup?

A haplogroup is a group of similar haplotypes sharing a common ancestor with a defining mutation — the branches of the Y-DNA and mtDNA trees.

Do you store my raw DNA data?

No. Raw reads and variant calls stay on your device or personal data store. Only anonymized computed summaries (coverage metrics, ancestry percentages, haplogroup calls) are aggregated for reporting.

How do I contribute?

Contributions are made through Navigator, which computes summaries locally and submits variant/branch proposals to curators for review and naming.

How do I sign in?

Use your AT Protocol account, or a local credential if your administrator issued one. App passwords are deprecated — see app-password help.
